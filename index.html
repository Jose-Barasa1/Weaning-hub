Founded in 1895, the International Cooperative Alliance (ICA) is the apex organisation uniting cooperative federations and organisations worldwide — representing over 3 million cooperatives and more than 1 billion members across 110 countries. Operating from a global office in Brussels, Belgium, the ICA is organised with four Regional Offices: Europe, Africa (Nairobi), Americas (San José), and Asia-Pacific (New Delhi).

About AMCCO — Africa's Premier Cooperative Policy Forum

The Africa Ministerial Cooperative Conference (AMCCO) is the premier continental intergovernmental platform that convenes Ministers responsible for Cooperatives across Africa to advance policy harmonization, peer learning, and strategic collaboration in strengthening the Cooperative movement.

Established to elevate Cooperatives as a central pillar of Africa's socio-economic transformation, AMCCO serves as a high-level policy forum shaping continental consensus on:

Cooperative Development
Financial Inclusion
Youth & Women Empowerment
Climate Resilience

Through its periodic Ministerial Conferences and Declarations, AMCCO articulates forward-looking policy commitments aligned with the African Union's Agenda 2063, the Sustainable Development Goals, and national development priorities across Member States.

Vision and Objectives

"A transformed African society where cooperatives are the preferred model for inclusive, sustainable, and people-centered socio-economic development."

Core Objectives

  • Advance Policy Harmonization — Promote the adoption and implementation of the AU Pan-African Model Law on Cooperatives across all member states
  • Foster Peer Learning — Create a platform for Ministers and cooperative leaders to share best practices, innovations, and lessons learned
  • Drive Strategic Collaboration — Facilitate partnerships between governments, the cooperative movement, and development partners to mobilise resources and co-finance priority programs
  • Strengthen Institutional Capacity — Ensure the AMCCO Secretariat (ICA-Africa) is adequately resourced to coordinate a continent-wide implementation agenda
  • Champion Inclusive Development — Embed mandatory targets for women and youth participation in cooperative governance, leadership, and economic opportunities

Recurring Themes in AMCCO Declarations

Across four decades of declarations, a consistent set of priorities emerges, reflecting the core challenges and opportunities facing Africa's cooperative movement.

🏛️ Governance & Policy Frameworks
Strong, well-governed cooperative systems. Countries are encouraged to adopt the AU Pan-African Model Law on Cooperatives.
💰 Financial Sustainability
SACCOs, cooperative banks, and insurance. Supportive government policies maintain stability and enable expansion.
👩‍🦱 Gender & Youth Inclusion
Declarations repeatedly highlight the need to address imbalances and create opportunities for women and young people.
📚 Capacity Building & Education
Education and training are the backbone of successful cooperatives. Investing in skills for leaders and members improves performance.
🌍 Regional Collaboration & Trade
Stronger partnerships among African countries enhance trade and knowledge sharing, aligned with Agenda 2063 and AfCFTA.
💡 Technology, Innovation & Research
ICT adoption improves efficiency and transparency. Investment in research systems generates insights to improve performance.
🌱 Community Development & Environment
Cooperatives are people-centred organisations. Declarations highlight their role in community development and climate action.

Building the Enabling Environment

Three foundational instruments now in place — the platform is set for continental-scale implementation.

📜 Model Cooperative Law for Africa
Launched October 2024 with Pan-African Parliament. A harmonised legislative framework for all 55 member states — the first instrument of its kind on the continent.

Priority: Popularise and support country-level legislative alignment
👩‍🦱 National Cooperative Gender Platform Framework
Launched May 2024. Advances meaningful participation of women in cooperative governance, leadership, and ownership.

Priority: Complete national chapter establishment across member states
📋 Nairobi Declaration
Adopted 2025. Ten-action, three-year short-term agenda — most results-oriented declaration in AMCCO history.

Priority: Structured implementation with transparent M&E reporting

The 2025 Nairobi Declaration — Ten Actions

Three-year short-term agenda (2025–2028) — Implementation monitored through Annual Technical Committee and midterm review at 18 months.

  • Popularise and support country-level adoption of the Model Cooperative Law for Africa
  • Advance inclusive cooperative development with mandatory targets for women and youth
  • Establish structured monitoring and evaluation of all AMCCO declaration commitments
  • Develop a coordinated, sustainable resource mobilisation strategy for AMCCO
  • Strengthen the AMCCO Secretariat (ICA-Africa) for continent-wide coordination
  • Advance cooperative integration into the African Continental Free Trade Area
  • Scale cooperative health, education, and care economy programmes across member states
  • Establish equitable AMCCO conference cost-sharing and hosting arrangements
  • Facilitate joint programming and co-financing with development partners
  • Build cooperative statistical capacity for evidence-based policy across Africa

Short-Term Priorities 2025–2028

Priority 1 — Legislative Harmonisation: Popularising the Model Cooperative Law
Country-by-country engagement to review national legislation against the model law. Peer learning exchanges. Targeted advocacy at regional economic communities, national parliaments, and the African Union.

Target: Majority of African member states formally engaged in legislative alignment by 2028
Priority 2 — Inclusive Cooperative Development: Women and Youth at the Centre
Complete establishment of national chapters under the Gender Platform Framework. Embed gender and youth inclusion targets. Increase proportion of women in cooperative leadership. Expand cooperative financing access for youth and women members.
